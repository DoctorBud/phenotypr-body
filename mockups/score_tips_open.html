Profile sufficiency: Very Poor The profile sufficiency rating represents how diagnostically useful your profile will be based on the symptoms you've added.

Tips for adding symptoms to help your doctor diagnose you most accurately

  • Enter as many symptoms as you can
  • Be as specific as you can be
  • Try to cover as many body symptoms as apply to you (e.g. symptoms in the head and neck, face, eyes, ears, muscles, joints, bones, internal organs, etc.)
  • Don't forget conditions that are not local to a specific body part (e.g. stroke, fever, numbness, sensitivity to pain, etc.)
  • Don't forget conditions like sleep disturbances, memory loss, cognitive disability, developmental delay
